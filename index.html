MacBook Air M2

Design and Display

The MacBook Air M2 features a sleek, lightweight design with a durable aluminum chassis. It has a 13.6-inch Liquid Retina display with vibrant colors, high brightness, and True Tone technology for a comfortable viewing experience. Its thin bezels and slim profile enhance portability without compromising screen quality.

Why should you buy this?

With its speed, efficiency, and durability, the MacBook Air M2 is a solid choice. When the M2 chip runs cool and never heats up, it outperforms most competitors. With a battery life of up to 18 hours, this device surpasses most laptops. Additionally, macOS is designed for speed and reliability, ensuring excellent performance and durability.

Macbook Air M1 vs Macbook Air M2

Both models come with 8GB of memory and 256GB of storage which can both be upgraded to 2TB, and have a price difference of around 100 USD. The macbook air M2 however, can support more RAM (Unified Memory) than the M1—up to 24GB compared to the M1’s 16GB. For graphics processing, the Air M1 comes with a 7-core GPU and the Air M2 comes with an 8-core GPU. If you want to future-proof your laptop, you should consider getting at least 16GB of memory and a 512GB storage drive. The memory and storage quantities will affect your day-to-day experience more than your choice of processor when comparing the M1 and M2.
